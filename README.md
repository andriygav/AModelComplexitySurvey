## From VC Dimension to Scaling Laws: The Evolution of Theoretical Approaches to Complexity and Generalization

[**Andrey Grabovoy**](https://intsystems.github.io/people/grabovoy_av/index.html)<br>
Institute of Control Sciences of the Russian Academy of Sciences
</div>

<br>

> **Abstract:** *Classical complexity measures such as VC dimension, Rademacher complexity, and PAC-Bayesian bounds provide the foundational language of statistical learning theory, yet they are often insufficient to explain the behavior of modern overparameterized deep networks.
> This survey presents the transition from hypothesis-class capacity analysis toward effective complexity perspectives that incorporate optimization dynamics, loss-landscape geometry, Hessian structure, training regimes, double descent, and scaling laws.
> The manuscript is organized as a scoping review with an explicit corpus-construction pipeline (OpenAlex, arXiv, Crossref; deduplication; semi-automated narrowing; expert screening), and synthesizes current literature into a multi-level explanatory framework for deep learning generalization.*
